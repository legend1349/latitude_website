Our Story

"Your postcode
isn't your potential."

Latitude was founded locally on a simple belief: where you grow up shouldn't decide how far you go. Too many students in Sydney's north-west have talent and drive but not the support that students at bigger schools or wealthier postcodes take for granted.

We built Latitude to close that gap. Not with online recordings or one-size-fits-all classes, but with the kind of focused, high-quality support that actually turns effort into results. Every student deserves a tutor who knows their name and remembers where they got stuck last week.

What We Offer

Our Core Programme

Group lessons
2 to 4 students

Our group programme is the heart of what we do. Every session runs with a maximum of four students, which means your child gets genuine attention, not a seat in a crowd.

Each week alternates between theory sessions, where new concepts from the HSC Mathematics syllabus are introduced and worked through properly, and homework-help sessions, where students bring their actual school tasks and get them sorted with direct guidance.

Sessions run weekly throughout the school term, keeping students on pace with their school programme and ahead of what is coming next.

One on One

Individual lessons

For students who want focused support outside the group setting, individual sessions are available on request.

These are particularly useful for targeted work before a major assessment, catching up after a period of absence, or working through a specific topic that needs more time than a group session allows.

Individual lessons can be taken alongside a group programme or as a standalone arrangement.

Latitude or a Private Tutor?

An honest comparison

Private tutors can be excellent. Some produce genuinely strong results. But private arrangements depend entirely on one person being available, prepared, and consistent for two full years. Life intervenes. Tutors fall ill, face exam periods of their own, or take on more students than they can manage. When that happens, there is no backup. You are simply without support.

At Latitude, sessions are backed by the centre, not just the individual. If something changes, a replacement is arranged. Your student does not lose momentum because a person had a bad week.

There is also the question of structure. A committed private tutor is excellent when they show up. But the HSC has a fixed syllabus, fixed timing, and fixed exam conditions. Latitude builds every session around that structure, so students are never guessing what was covered, and nothing important gets quietly skipped.
